Menu

Shoyu Ramen

醤油ラーメン

PRICE: 123 SEK

Shoyu ramen is the most common type of ramen. It has a clear brown broth, based on a chicken and vegetable (or sometimes fish or beef) stock with plenty of soy sauce added resulting in a soup that is tangy, salty, and savory yet still fairly light on the palate. Shoyu ramen is typically topped with menma, green onions, nori, and chashu.

Miso Ramen

味噌ラーメン

PRICE: 123 SEK

Miso ramen is a relative newcomer, having reached national prominence around 1965. This uniquely Japanese ramen, which was developed in Hokkaido, features a broth that combines copious miso and is blended with oily chicken or fish broth - and sometimes with tonkotsu or lard - to create a thick, nutty, slightly sweet and very hearty soup.

Tonkotsu Ramen

豚骨ラーメン

PRICE: 123 SEK

Tonkotsu ramen usually has a cloudy white colored broth. It is similar to the Chinese baitang and has a thick broth made from boiling pork bones, fat, and collagen over high heat for many hours, which suffuses the broth with a hearty pork flavor and a creamy consistency that rivals milk, melted butter or gravy (depending on the shop).

Spicy miso Ramen

辛味噌ラーメン

PRICE: 123 SEK

Spicy Miso Ramen features a thick, yellow broth made from a soybean-based miso paste, offering a rich and savory flavor. This type of ramen is typically served with thicker noodles and a loose boiled egg, along with other toppings such as beef or pork, green onions, and sometimes mushrooms. The addition of spicy elements enhances the flavor profile of the dish, providing a satisfying and comforting meal

Shio Ramen

塩ラーメン

PRICE: 123 SEK

Shio Ramen is a popular and common type of ramen in Japan. It is made from a delicate chicken-based broth with a muted salty flavor, providing a delicious and light soup. The name "shio" comes from the Japanese word for salt, as salt is added to the chicken soup and dashi base to create the distinctive flavor. This ramen is often topped with simple ingredients such as bamboo shoots, sliced chashu (chicken or pork), and eggs.

Tsukemen Ramen

つけ麺ラーメン

PRICE: 123 SEK

Tsukemen is a type of ramen dish in Japanese cuisine where the noodles are typically served cold, while the soup is typically served hot, which serves to season and moisten the noodles. The soup base is usually thicker and more concentrated than ramen so it sticks to the noodles. The toppings are typically served atop or on the side within the dish of soup, such as nori, chashu, menma, tamago, and eggs.
